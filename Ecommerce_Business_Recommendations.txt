TOP 5 BUSINESS INSIGHTS & RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================

INSIGHT 1 — Extreme Geographic Revenue Concentration
----------------------------------------------------------------
Finding:
São Paulo (SP) state alone generates $5.2M in revenue — 39% of 
total company revenue ($13.2M) — from a single state, while the 
next two states (RJ, MG) combined contribute only 26%.

Root Cause:
Olist's seller and customer base is heavily concentrated in 
Brazil's economic hub, with limited market penetration elsewhere.

Recommendation:
- Launch targeted marketing campaigns in underpenetrated states 
  (e.g., MA, CE, BA) where order volume is low but growth potential exists
- Diversify seller onboarding incentives outside SP to reduce 
  single-region dependency
- Monitor SP-specific risks (regulatory, logistics, competition) 
  given its outsized revenue contribution

Expected Impact:
Reducing SP's revenue share from 39% to 30% by growing other 
regions could protect against regional business disruption and 
unlock an estimated 15-20% incremental national revenue.

================================================================

INSIGHT 2 — High-Value Orders Face Greater Delivery Risk
----------------------------------------------------------------
Finding:
Late-delivered orders carry a higher average value ($135.71) than 
on-time orders ($118.87) — a 14% premium. "Not Delivered" orders 
show the highest average value of all at $151.08.

Root Cause:
Higher-value orders may involve more items, larger/heavier products, 
or longer-distance shipments — all of which increase logistics 
complexity and delay risk.

Recommendation:
- Implement priority/expedited handling for orders above a defined 
  value threshold (e.g., $150+)
- Add proactive delivery tracking and customer communication for 
  high-value shipments
- Review carrier performance specifically for high-ticket orders

Expected Impact:
Protecting high-value order delivery could prevent disproportionate 
revenue loss, given these orders contribute more per transaction 
to overall revenue.

================================================================

INSIGHT 3 — Maranhão (MA) Has the Worst Delivery Performance
----------------------------------------------------------------
Finding:
MA state shows a 16.73% late delivery rate — nearly 2.5x the 
company-wide average of 6.77%. Other underperforming states 
include CE (13.17%) and BA (11.72%).

Root Cause:
Likely longer shipping distances from primary fulfillment/seller 
hubs (concentrated in SP/RJ) combined with less developed regional 
logistics infrastructure.

Recommendation:
- Audit carrier and route performance specifically for MA, CE, and BA
- Explore regional fulfillment partnerships or local warehousing 
  to reduce delivery distance
- Set more realistic estimated delivery dates for high-risk states 
  to better align customer expectations

Expected Impact:
Improving logistics for these 3 states could reduce their combined 
late delivery rate by an estimated 5-7 percentage points.

================================================================

INSIGHT 4 — Credit Card Dominates Payment Behavior
----------------------------------------------------------------
Finding:
Credit card is used in 73.9% of all transactions, representing 
$12.5M (nearly 95%) of total payment value, with an average of 
3 installments per transaction.

Root Cause:
Installment-based credit card payments are a culturally preferred 
and accessible payment method in the Brazilian e-commerce market.

Recommendation:
- Ensure credit card payment infrastructure has maximum reliability 
  and minimal transaction friction, given its outsized importance
- Test installment-based promotional offers (e.g., "0% interest, 
  6 installments") to drive higher cart values
- Monitor boleto (19% of transactions) users separately, as this 
  payment method has different cash-flow timing implications

Expected Impact:
Optimizing the credit card checkout experience could reduce cart 
abandonment and support higher average order values across the 
dominant payment channel.

================================================================

INSIGHT 5 — Top Category Concentration in Revenue
----------------------------------------------------------------
Finding:
The top 3 categories (Health & Beauty, Watches & Gifts, Bed/Bath/
Table) generate $3.5M combined — about 27% of total revenue — out 
of 70+ product categories. Watches & Gifts shows the highest 
average order value among top categories despite fewer orders 
than Bed/Bath/Table.

Root Cause:
Premium-positioned categories (Watches & Gifts) drive higher 
revenue per transaction, while high-volume categories (Bed/Bath/
Table) rely on transaction frequency rather than price.

Recommendation:
- Expand premium/gift-category catalog depth, given strong revenue-
  per-order performance
- Apply volume-driven promotional strategy (bundling, free shipping 
  thresholds) for high-frequency categories like Bed/Bath/Table
- Investigate low-performing categories (e.g., ferramentas_jardim) 
  for potential catalog rationalization or targeted promotion

Expected Impact:
A focused dual strategy (premium expansion + volume promotion) 
could improve overall category-level profitability without 
requiring uniform across-the-board investment.
